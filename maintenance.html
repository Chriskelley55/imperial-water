The Case For Regular Service

Filters Don't Last Forever

An expired filter doesn't just stop working — it can become a breeding ground for bacteria. Don't let your water protection expire quietly.

1

Filters Expire

Most sediment, carbon, and RO filters last 6–12 months depending on usage. After that, they're not just ineffective — they can harbor bacteria and release contaminants back into the water.

2

Systems Drift

Pressure drops, connections loosen, and parts wear. A quick inspection every 6–12 months catches small problems before they become expensive repairs.

3

Water Changes

Municipal water quality shifts seasonally. Well water can change after heavy rain. Regular testing ensures your system is still matched to your actual water.

Every Visit

What We Do When We Show Up

Filter Replacement

Every filter stage swapped with OEM-quality replacements. Sediment, carbon block, and RO membrane if due.

Pressure & Flow Testing

We check inlet pressure, flow rate, and system output. Low flow is often the first sign of a failing membrane or clogged pre-filter.

Water Quality Check

Quick on-site test for hardness, pH, and TDS (total dissolved solids) — before and after the system. Numbers don't lie.

Full System Inspection

Every connection, valve, and tank inspected for wear, leaks, or anything that needs attention.

Written Service Report

You get a written summary of what was done, what was replaced, and what to watch for. Kept on file for your records.
