## 2. Reviews — talking points that aren't generic

When you ask a happy customer for a Google review, the difference between a 5-word review ("great service!") and a 50-word review (specific, with names) is huge for SEO and conversion. The trick is giving them a tiny prompt so they don't have to think.

**Five things customers actually care about that you can prompt them on:**

1. **The water test / honest diagnosis** — "He didn't try to upsell, just tested and told me what I needed."
2. **Speed** — "Showed up next day, fixed it in an hour."
3. **The clean install** — "Didn't tear up my garage / left the area cleaner than he found it."
4. **You picking up the phone** — "I texted on a Sunday, got a reply in 10 minutes."
5. **Local / family operation** — "Glad I went with a local guy instead of one of the big chains."

**Copy-paste text you can send a happy customer 24 hrs after the job:**

> Hey [name], hope the new system is treating you well! If you've got 30 seconds, would mean a lot if you could leave a quick Google review. Even one sentence about [the install / the diagnosis / how fast we got out there] helps a ton. Here's the link: [your Google review link]
>
> Thanks again — Kurt

If you want me to set up the auto-text version of this (sends the day after every completed install, link prefilled), that's the **$200 review automation** add-on. Otherwise totally fine to do it by hand — the manual version actually converts better for the first ~50 reviews because it feels personal.
